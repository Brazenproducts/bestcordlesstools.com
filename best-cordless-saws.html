Best Cordless Saws of 2026

Circular, reciprocating, and miter saws — all running on batteries

Cordless saws used to be a joke. Underpowered, short runtime, couldn't cut through a 2x4 without bogging down. That era is over. Modern brushless motors and high-output batteries have made cordless saws genuinely competitive with corded models. Some are even better — no cord to trip over, no generator needed on remote jobsites.

But saws are the most demanding cordless tools. They draw massive current and expose every weakness in a battery platform. That's why choosing the right saw matters more than almost any other tool category. Here are our picks.

The Bottom Line on Cordless Saws

Circular saws: DeWalt FLEXVOLT is the king. If you're in the Milwaukee ecosystem, the M18 FUEL is excellent but can't quite match FLEXVOLT's sustained power. Reciprocating saws: Milwaukee SAWZALL is the best, but DeWalt's XR is 90% of the performance at a better price. Miter saws: Cordless miter saws are still a niche — great for trim work and portability, but don't sell your corded 12" slider yet.

Saw Buying Tip

Cordless saws drain batteries fast. Budget for at least 2 high-capacity batteries (5.0Ah+) if you plan to use a cordless saw regularly.
